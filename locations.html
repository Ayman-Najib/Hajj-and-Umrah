The Complete Hajj Guide

Meaning of Hajj: Hajj is the pilgrimage to the holy city of Mecca in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, where Muslims perform a series of prescribed spiritual rituals at specific times.

Importance of Hajj:

  • Hajj is one of the five pillars of Islam and is obligatory for every adult, physically and financially capable Muslim to perform it at least once in their lifetime.
  • Hajj promotes unity and connection among Muslims from different countries and cultures.
  • Hajj is an opportunity for repentance, spiritual purification, and personal renewal.

Key Pillars of Hajj:

  • Ihram: Entering a state of consecration by wearing the prescribed attire and declaring the intention for Hajj.
  • Standing at Arafah: Gathering in the plain of Arafah on the 9th day of Dhul-Hijjah for prayers and supplications.
  • Tawaf: Circumambulating the Kaaba seven times.
  • Sa'i: Walking between the hills of Safa and Marwa seven times.
  • Stoning of the Jamarat: Symbolically stoning the pillars representing Satan.

A Detailed Guide to Performing Umrah

1. Preparations:

  • Ensure you meet the health and financial requirements.
  • Plan your travel, accommodation, and transportation.
  • Familiarize yourself with the rules and instructions for Umrah.

2. Arrival in Mecca & Rituals:

  • Entering Ihram: Enter the state of Ihram at the designated Miqat point.
  • Tawaf of the Kaaba: Perform Tawaf by circling the Kaaba seven times.
  • Sa'i between Safa and Marwa: Perform Sa'i by walking seven times between the two hills.

3. Completion:

  • After completing the rituals, men shave or shorten their hair, and women trim a small amount of their hair to exit the state of Ihram.
